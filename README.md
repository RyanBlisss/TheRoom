The Room

Main Idea:
Audio-Based Atmospheric Horror 2D Game
The Audio is more important than the visual in this game
AI generated songs in the background adding even more atmospheric feel
Hotel with locked rooms
To get to the exit you need to unlock all the doors before exiting as only the last room has the exit key
Rooms will change when you unlock another room, you’ll never know where the next room key is… Unless you go look
Your sanity will permanently decrease by 20% after the rooms change. The sanity pills can only heal non-permanent sanity(sanity drop from time), but not this one
The rooms get scarier and scarier the more rooms you unlock, you’ll see many different things every time, new and old(somewhere else)
To exit you need to unlock all the rooms. The final room is called “The Room” because it will be really challenging to get including a face to face fight off. If you manage to get away from there, it’ll be the most challenging to escape
This game has a whole entire Story behind it

Story
Prologue: Ocean Breeze, Fading Light
You are Blarg Thompson, on what was supposed to be the ultimate getaway. The Florida sun beats down as you step off the shuttle and through the gilded doors of the Oceancrest Hotel. The lobby’s white marble gleams; a soft steel-blue wave motif ripples across the walls. You claim your room key and follow the carpeted hallway, already tasting salt on the air.

Scene I: The Perfect Room
You push open the door. Sunlight floods in through sliding glass doors, illuminating a plush queen bed draped in crisp white linens. To your right, a curved leather couch faces a wall-mounted flatscreen. On the balcony, two Adirondack chairs cradle a panoramic view of turquoise waves and ivory sand. Laughter drifts up from the beach below.
You drop your bag and slide onto the couch, closing your eyes. Inhale: sunscreen coconut, sea salt, fresh linen. Exhale: total relaxation.

Scene II: Beachside Reverie
Ten minutes later, you’re on the warm sand. A DJ’s pulsing beat sets a carefree rhythm, and string lights arc overhead in a half-circle of pastel bulbs. A troupe of fire-twirlers practices nearby; the scent of grilled mahi-mahi drifts in from a beachfront tiki bar. You lie back and let the sun’s rays seep through your shirt, warming every muscle.
As you sit up to catch some water from your backpack, you pat your pockets—no sunscreen. You shrug. “Only been here a few minutes,” you think. “I’ll be fine.” You head back up the wooden promenade toward your suite.

Scene III: Unnerving Silence
You step through the lobby doors… and everything falls still. The tropical breeze is gone. The scent of salt is muted. The distant bass of the party has vanished.
The front desk’s reception lamps are dark; not a sole behind the counter. The bell jars and brochures are untouched, as if no one had been here all day. You call out, “Hello?” Your voice echoes unnaturally down the empty hallway.
A flicker of movement by the elevators—shadows—yet no attendant emerges. You pause, unease prickling at your neck.

Scene IV: Lights Out
You mount the stairs two at a time, throat tightening. When you reach your door, you fumble through your bag and retrieve your sunscreen—only to have every light in the corridor—and your room—snap off at once.
Complete darkness. The air grows thick, humid in a way that feels… stifling. Your phone’s flashlight beam quivers across the doorframe.
A low metallic clink echoes behind you. You whirl—nothing but the empty corridor. You whisper, “Okay, weird power surge,” and step inside.

Scene V: First Ominous Clue
On the bedside table sits a folded piece of luxury hotel stationery you’ve never seen:
Dear Guest,
We hope you’re enjoying your stay. For your safety, please remain in your room until further notice.
—Management
Nothing about a maintenance notice mentioned that. No scrawl of a signature, no official stamp—just that unsettling, impersonal tone. You scan the hallway through the peephole: still dark, still silent.

Scene VI: The Whispering Vent
You cross to the balcony door—and hesitate. Behind you, the vents over the closet begin to whisper: a soft susurrus of indistinct voices.
You press your ear to the grille:
“…don’t trust the lights…”
“…he sees you…”
Your heart hammers. You grip the door’s handle, ready to fling it open and sprint to the lobby—to anywhere but here.

Scene VII: Turning Point
This was supposed to be a simple holiday—sun, sea, and ZERO stress. But now, with the lights out and that opaque note in your hand, the hotel has become something else: a cage.
You slip the sunscreen into your pocket. Whatever’s waiting in these darkened halls, you’ll need every resource you’ve got—logical thinking, a calm mind… and maybe, just maybe, a bit of that bygone holiday optimism.
